Introduction

Beyond the well-known rainforests and savannas lie hidden habitats — mangroves, peatlands, caves, and cloud forests. These ecosystems are often overlooked, yet they play vital roles in biodiversity and climate regulation.

Mangroves

Found along tropical coastlines, mangroves protect shorelines from erosion, provide nurseries for fish, and store massive amounts of carbon.

Peatlands

Peatlands are wetlands rich in organic matter. They cover only 3% of Earth’s land but store twice as much carbon as all forests combined.

Cloud Forests

Nestled in mountain ranges, cloud forests are shrouded in mist. They host rare orchids, amphibians, and birds found nowhere else.

Caves

Underground caves harbor unique species adapted to darkness, from blind fish to bioluminescent insects.

“Hidden habitats remind us that life thrives in the most unexpected corners of our planet.”
