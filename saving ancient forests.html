Introduction

Ancient forests are more than just trees — they are living archives of biodiversity, culture, and history. These ecosystems, some millions of years old, provide shelter to countless species and regulate the Earth’s climate.

Why They Matter

Forests act as the lungs of our planet, absorbing carbon dioxide and releasing oxygen. They are home to indigenous communities, rare wildlife, and medicinal plants that sustain human health.

Threats

Logging, mining, and agricultural expansion are destroying these irreplaceable ecosystems. Once lost, ancient forests cannot be recreated — their biodiversity and cultural heritage vanish forever.

Global Efforts

Conservationists, governments, and local communities are working together to protect these forests. Initiatives include creating protected areas, promoting sustainable forestry, and empowering indigenous guardians.

How You Can Help

  • Support organizations dedicated to forest conservation.
  • Reduce paper and wood consumption.
  • Raise awareness about the importance of ancient forests.
  • Advocate for stronger environmental policies.
