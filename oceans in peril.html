Introduction

Oceans cover more than 70% of our planet and sustain billions of lives. Yet today, they face unprecedented threats from human activity and climate change.

Plastic Pollution

Millions of tons of plastic enter the oceans each year, harming marine animals that ingest or become entangled in debris. Microplastics now permeate the food chain, affecting both wildlife and humans.

Warming Seas

Rising global temperatures are heating the oceans, leading to coral bleaching, shifting fish populations, and more frequent extreme weather events.

Ocean Acidification

Increased carbon dioxide absorption is lowering ocean pH, weakening shells of marine organisms and disrupting ecosystems that depend on them.

Global Efforts

International treaties, marine protected areas, and grassroots cleanup campaigns are working to restore ocean health. Scientists and activists emphasize the urgency of reducing emissions and plastic use.

“Protecting the oceans means protecting life itself.”

How You Can Help

  • Reduce single-use plastics and recycle responsibly.
  • Support organizations dedicated to ocean conservation.
  • Advocate for stronger climate and pollution policies.
  • Choose sustainable seafood options.
