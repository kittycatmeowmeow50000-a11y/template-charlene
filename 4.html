Introduction

Around the world, communities are facing the direct impacts of climate change. Rising seas, droughts, and extreme weather events are reshaping lives and landscapes. These are the climate frontlines.

Rising Seas

Coastal villages are losing land to encroaching tides. Families are forced to relocate, and cultural heritage sites risk being submerged forever.

Drought and Scarcity

Farmers in arid regions struggle with failing crops and water shortages. Innovative irrigation and community resilience projects are helping them adapt.

Extreme Weather

Hurricanes, floods, and wildfires are becoming more frequent and intense. Communities on the frontlines are rebuilding stronger, but the challenges grow each year.

Voices of Resilience

Indigenous leaders, youth activists, and local innovators are raising awareness and driving solutions. Their voices remind us that climate change is not a distant threat — it is here and now.

“On the climate frontlines, survival is innovation, and resilience is hope.”

How You Can Help

  • Support frontline communities through donations and advocacy.
  • Reduce your carbon footprint by choosing sustainable practices.
  • Amplify stories from those most affected by climate change.
  • Push for stronger climate policies at local and global levels.
